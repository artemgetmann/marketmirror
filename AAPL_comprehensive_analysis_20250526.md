## 2. Recent News

**Regulatory Issues and Tariffs**

President Donald Trump has threatened to impose a 25% tariff on Apple products if the company does not relocate iPhone manufacturing to the United States. This announcement led to a decline of over 2% in Apple's stock price at the opening of Wall Street. Trump criticized Apple's plans to shift production from China to India, emphasizing his preference for domestic manufacturing. The proposed tariffs are expected to take effect by the end of June. ([elpais.com](https://elpais.com/economia/2025-05-23/trump-amenaza-a-apple-con-aranceles-del-25-si-no-fabrica-el-iphone-en-estados-unidos.html?utm_source=openai))

In response, Apple is expanding its manufacturing operations in India. Foxconn, Apple's primary contractor, is investing $1.5 billion in a display module plant near Chennai and establishing a $450 million semiconductor assembly unit in Uttar Pradesh in partnership with HCL Technologies. This strategy aims to diversify Apple's supply chain away from China. ([ft.com](https://www.ft.com/content/7fdb8aaa-9296-4c57-a851-13db560a253f?utm_source=openai))

**Product Developments**

Apple plans to launch AI-enabled smart glasses by the end of 2026. These glasses are expected to feature a camera, microphone, speakers, and integration with Apple's Siri voice assistant. This move is part of Apple's efforts to enhance its artificial intelligence strategy and compete with Meta's Ray-Ban smart glasses. ([axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2025/05/22/apple-ai-glasses-2026?utm_source=openai))

**Pricing Strategies**

Apple is considering raising prices for its upcoming fall iPhone lineup. The company aims to avoid attributing the potential price hikes to U.S. tariffs on Chinese imports, despite most of its devices being assembled in China. The new iPhone models are projected to be priced up to $1,142, a 43% increase over the current $799 entry price. Analysts caution that this price increase could result in lost market share to competitors like Samsung. ([reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/business/apple-considers-raising-iphone-prices-wsj-reports-2025-05-12/?utm_source=openai))
