## 2. Recent News

**Regulatory Issues:**

- **EU Compliance:** In January 2024, Apple announced significant changes to its App Store and services to comply with European Union competition laws. Effective March 7, 2024, these changes allow iOS users in the EU to utilize alternative app stores and payment methods within apps, marking a substantial shift in Apple's ecosystem. ([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Inc.?utm_source=openai))

- **DOJ Settlement:** In November 2023, Apple agreed to a $25 million settlement with the U.S. Department of Justice over allegations of discriminatory hiring practices against U.S. citizens. The case involved job postings that were not publicly listed and required paper applications, primarily targeting foreign workers for PERM recruitment. ([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Inc.?utm_source=openai))

**Layoffs:**

- **Bay Area Reductions:** In late 2024, Apple conducted its first significant layoffs since the pandemic, affecting hundreds of employees in the Bay Area. This move reflects broader industry trends of workforce optimization amid economic uncertainties. ([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Apple_Inc.?utm_source=openai))

**Product Launches:**

- **Apple Intelligence:** In June 2024, Apple introduced "Apple Intelligence," integrating on-device artificial intelligence capabilities across its product lineup. This initiative underscores Apple's commitment to enhancing user experience through advanced AI technologies. ([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Inc.?utm_source=openai))

- **Pixelmator Acquisition:** On November 1, 2024, Apple acquired Pixelmator, a company renowned for its image editing applications for iPhone and Mac. This acquisition aims to bolster Apple's software offerings, particularly in creative applications. ([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Inc.?utm_source=openai))

**Legal Settlements:**

- **Siri Privacy Lawsuit:** On December 31, 2024, Apple agreed to a $95 million settlement in a lawsuit alleging that its Siri assistant unlawfully recorded private conversations through unintentional activations and shared them with third parties. Affected users may claim up to $20 per device. ([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Inc.?utm_source=openai))
