## 2. Recent News

**Layoffs and Reorganization**

In August 2024, Apple eliminated approximately 100 positions within its digital services division, primarily affecting teams responsible for the Apple Books app and Apple Bookstore. This restructuring also impacted engineering roles and the Apple News team. The layoffs are part of Apple's broader reorganization efforts, focusing on new priorities such as artificial intelligence. Earlier in 2024, Apple halted work on a high-end Vision headset and ceased development of its own smartwatch displays. Additionally, the company faced a 6.5% decline in sales in China, its third-largest market, during the previous quarter. ([reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/technology/apple-cuts-about-100-jobs-its-services-division-bloomberg-news-reports-2024-08-28/?utm_source=openai))

**Regulatory Challenges**

In November 2023, Apple agreed to a $25 million settlement with the U.S. Department of Justice over allegations of discriminatory hiring practices. The company was accused of creating job postings that were not publicly listed and required paper applications, primarily targeting foreign workers as part of the PERM recruitment process. Apple denied any wrongdoing but settled the case to resolve the allegations. ([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Inc.?utm_source=openai))

**Product Developments**

In June 2024, at the Worldwide Developers Conference (WWDC), Apple announced the introduction of "Apple Intelligence," incorporating on-device artificial intelligence capabilities into its products. This initiative aims to enhance features like Siri by integrating advanced AI functionalities. The first product to feature these improvements is the iPhone 15 Pro running iOS 18. The announcement led to a temporary increase in Apple's stock price, briefly making it the world's most valuable company ahead of Microsoft. ([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Apple_Inc.?utm_source=openai))
