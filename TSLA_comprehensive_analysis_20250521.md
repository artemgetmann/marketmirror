## 2. Recent News

**Regulatory Issues**

Tesla's plan to launch a limited fleet of robotaxis in Austin, Texas, by June 2025 has attracted heightened scrutiny from the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA). The agency is requesting more transparency and details about the company's autonomous vehicle plans, particularly concerning safety measures and performance in low-visibility conditions. This follows ongoing investigations into Tesla's Full Self-Driving (FSD) system due to concerns over its performance. ([axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2025/05/21/tesla-robotaxi-musk?utm_source=openai))

**Product Launches**

CEO Elon Musk announced that Tesla is on track to begin a robotaxi trial in Austin by the end of June 2025. The initial phase will involve around 10 self-driving vehicles operating in designated, geofenced areas, with plans to scale up to approximately 1,000 vehicles within a few months. This initiative marks a strategic shift, as Musk has deprioritized launching a more affordable EV in favor of focusing on robotaxis and the Optimus humanoid robots. ([reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/business/autos-transportation/muREDACTED_API_KEY/?utm_source=openai))

**Demand Rebound**

Musk reported a "major rebound in demand" for Tesla vehicles, following a period of declining sales in the first quarter of 2025. This resurgence is attributed to Musk's reduced involvement in political activities, which had previously impacted consumer confidence. ([axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2025/05/20/elon-musk-tesla-ev-sales-doge?utm_source=openai))

**Public Perception**

Tesla's brand reputation has declined significantly, falling to 95th in the Axios Harris Poll 100. This drop is largely attributed to Musk's political activism, which has influenced public perception and, consequently, demand for Tesla vehicles. ## 5. Final Recommendation

**Recommendation: Hold**

Tesla's current valuation reflects high growth expectations, supported by recent developments in autonomous driving and a rebound in vehicle demand. However, the company faces significant regulatory scrutiny, particularly concerning its Full Self-Driving system and upcoming robotaxi initiatives. Additionally, public perception challenges linked to CEO Elon Musk's political activities may impact brand reputation and sales.

Given these factors, a cautious approach is warranted. Investors should monitor regulatory developments, the execution of Tesla's autonomous vehicle strategy, and shifts in public sentiment. While the company's long-term prospects remain promising, the current high valuation suggests limited immediate upside. Therefore, maintaining a hold position is advisable until clearer signals of sustained growth and regulatory compliance emerge.
