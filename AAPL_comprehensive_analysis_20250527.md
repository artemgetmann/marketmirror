## 2. Recent News

**Regulatory Issues:**

- **App Store Compliance with EU Regulations:** In January 2024, Apple announced significant changes to its App Store policies to comply with European Union competition laws. Effective March 7, 2024, these changes allow iOS users in the EU to access alternative app stores and use different payment methods within apps. This move aims to address regulatory scrutiny and enhance user choice. ([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Inc.?utm_source=openai))

**Layoffs:**

- **Digital Services Division Reductions:** In August 2024, Apple eliminated approximately 100 positions within its digital services group, primarily affecting teams responsible for the Apple Books app and Apple Bookstore. Some engineering roles and positions within the Apple News team were also impacted. This restructuring reflects Apple's ongoing efforts to realign resources amid shifting priorities, including a focus on artificial intelligence. ([reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/technology/apple-cuts-about-100-jobs-its-services-division-bloomberg-news-reports-2024-08-28/?utm_source=openai))

**Product Developments:**

- **Introduction of Apple Intelligence:** In June 2024, Apple unveiled "Apple Intelligence," integrating on-device artificial intelligence capabilities into its ecosystem. This initiative signifies Apple's commitment to enhancing user experiences through advanced AI features. ([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Inc.?utm_source=openai))

- **Acquisition of Pixelmator:** On November 1, 2024, Apple acquired Pixelmator, a company renowned for its image editing applications for iPhone and Mac. This acquisition is expected to bolster Apple's software offerings, particularly in the realm of creative tools. ([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Inc.?utm_source=openai))

**Legal Matters:**

- **Siri Privacy Settlement:** On December 31, 2024, Apple agreed to a $95 million settlement in a lawsuit alleging that its Siri assistant unlawfully recorded private conversations through unintentional activations and shared them with third parties. While denying any wrongdoing, Apple settled to resolve the claims, allowing affected users to potentially receive up to $20 per device. ([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Inc.?utm_source=openai))
