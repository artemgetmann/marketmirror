## 2. Recent News

**Regulatory Issues:**
In January 2024, Apple announced compliance with the European Union's competition law, implementing significant changes to the App Store and other services effective March 7. These changes allow iOS users in the EU to utilize alternative app stores and payment methods within apps, and introduce a menu in Safari for downloading alternative browsers like Chrome or Firefox. ([Source))

**Layoffs:**
In April 2024, Apple laid off over 600 employees associated with its electric car project and microLED development. These layoffs followed the reported shutdown of these projects in the preceding months. ([Source))

**Product Launches:**
In June 2024, at the Worldwide Developers Conference (WWDC), Apple introduced "Apple Intelligence," incorporating on-device artificial intelligence capabilities into its products. This initiative aims to enhance features like Siri by integrating advanced AI functionalities. ([Source))

**Acquisitions:**
On November 1, 2024, Apple announced the acquisition of Pixelmator, a company renowned for its image editing applications for iPhone and Mac. Pixelmator's apps have been previously highlighted during Apple's product launches, including being named Mac App of the Year in 2018. The acquisition is expected to bolster Apple's software offerings in creative applications. ([Source))

**Legal Settlements:**
On December 31, 2024, Apple agreed to a $95 million settlement in a lawsuit alleging that its Siri assistant unlawfully recorded private conversations through unintentional activations and shared them with third parties, including advertisers. While denying any wrongdoing, Apple settled the case, allowing affected users to potentially claim up to $20 per device. ([Source))
